## Watering Schedule Recommendation

| Setting | Recommendation | Notes |
|---------|---------------|-------|
| **Time of day** | 6:00–7:00 AM | ⚠ Do NOT water in the evening in North Alabama. Wet soil overnight in humid AL summers causes fungal disease on tomatoes and root rot. Morning watering lets soil absorb moisture before heat hits and any surface moisture evaporates quickly. |
| **Frequency** | Daily in peak summer (Jul–Aug) | Scale back to every other day in spring and fall |
| **Duration** | 20–30 minutes per zone | Adjust based on soil moisture — push a finger 2 in into soil. If dry, run longer. |

## Above Ground vs Buried Sections

### Buried sections (3 total)
All three buried sections cross gaps or walkable paths where above-ground line would be a trip hazard or get damaged.

- **East arch path** (~20 ft) — Branch A runs through the east arch path between Beds 2 & 4. Dig a trench 4–6 inches deep the full length. Use rigid PVC for this section.
- **Gap between Bed 2 and Bed 1** (~2 ft) — Short crossing at the top. A flat spade slice is all you need. Use rigid PVC.
- **Gap between Bed 4 and Bed 3** (~2 ft) — Same as above. Use rigid PVC.

### Above-ground sections
All remaining sections clip to bed walls with line clips every 12 inches. Use flexible ½" poly line for all above-ground runs.

### Transition points
At each point where buried PVC meets above-ground poly, use a ½" barbed straight coupler — push the poly line onto one barb and the PVC onto the other, clamp both with stainless hose clamps.

## Installation Steps

### Step 1 — Hose bib assembly
Attach in this exact order directly to the hose bib:
1. Timer
2. Backflow preventer
3. Filter
4. Pressure regulator
5. ½" poly main line

### Step 2 — Run main line to garden
- Run ½" poly from hose bib to the SE corner of Bed 4 (~5 ft) — install Y-splitter + T4 here
- **Branch A:**
  - Dig trench 4–6 in deep north through the east arch path from Bed 4 to Bed 2 (~20 ft)
  - Lay rigid PVC in trench, backfill, connect to poly at each end with barbed couplers
  - Run poly west along north perimeter of Bed 2 — install T2 along the way — 90° elbow at NW corner of Bed 2
  - Dig short trench across gap between Bed 2 and Bed 1, lay PVC, connect to poly
  - Run poly south down east edge of Bed 1 — install T1 along the way
  - End cap at SE corner of Bed 1
- **Branch B:**
  - Run poly west along south perimeter of Bed 4
  - Dig short trench across gap between Bed 4 and Bed 3, lay PVC, connect to poly
  - Run poly north up east edge of Bed 3 — install T3 along the way
  - End cap at NE corner of Bed 3
- Secure all above-ground poly to bed walls with line clips every 12 inches

### Step 3 — Install bed header lines
- Cut four 72-inch lengths of ½" poly line — one per bed
- Run along the back inside edge of each bed
- Connect to manifold splitter with barbed fittings
- Cap the far end of each header line

### Step 4 — Install row drip lines
- Use hole punch to punch holes in the header line at each row position (3 holes per bed = 3 rows)
- Insert barbed tee fittings at each hole
- Run ¼" drip line from each tee along the row length (72 inches)
- Cap the end of each drip line

### Step 5 — Install emitters
- Punch holes in the ¼" drip line at each plant location
- Insert adjustable emitters
- Push emitter stakes into soil next to each plant stem
- Set emitter flow rate:
  - Tomatoes, cucumbers, ginger: 1 GPH
  - Beans, okra, peppers, basil: 0.5 GPH
  - Herbs (thyme, oregano, rosemary): 0.25 GPH

### Step 6 — Set timer
- Recommended: **6:00 AM, daily, 25 minutes**
- Adjust duration after first week based on soil moisture
- Reduce to every other day in spring and fall when temps are below 80°F

### Step 7 — Test and adjust
- Turn system on manually and walk each bed
- Check every emitter is flowing
- Look for any gushing fittings (too much pressure) or no-flow emitters (clogged)
- Use goof plugs to seal any unused holes
- Lay 2–3 inches of straw mulch over drip lines to slow evaporation

## Winterizing (November)

1. Turn off timer and remove batteries
2. Disconnect system from hose bib
3. Open all end caps and blow out lines with a bicycle pump or compressor
4. Store timer, filter, pressure regulator, and backflow preventer indoors
5. Leave poly lines and emitters in place — they tolerate freezing fine once drained
6. Reconnect in April when planting season begins

## Watering Guide by Plant

| Plant | Weekly Water Need | Notes |
|-------|------------------|-------|
| Tomatoes | 1–2 inches | Deep and consistent — irregular watering causes blossom end rot |
| Cucumbers | 1 inch | Keep consistent — stress causes bitter fruit |
| Bell Peppers | 1 inch | Drought-tolerant once established but fruits better with consistent water |
| Pole Beans | ¾ inch | Don't overwater — causes root rot |
| Okra | ½ inch | Very drought-tolerant, less is more once established |
| Ginger | 1–1.5 inches | Loves consistent moisture, never let it fully dry out |
| Basil | ¾ inch | Let top inch of soil dry between waterings |
| Rosemary | ¼ inch | Drought-tolerant perennial — overwatering kills it faster than drought |
| Thyme / Oregano | ¼ inch | Same as rosemary — very drought tolerant |
| Nasturtium | ½ inch | Actually blooms better with slight stress — don't overwater |
| Dill | ½ inch | Consistent moisture, don't let it dry completely |
| Mint | 1 inch | Loves moisture — one of the thirstiest herbs |

## Troubleshooting

| Problem | Likely Cause | Fix |
|---------|-------------|-----|
| Emitter not flowing | Clogged | Remove and soak in white vinegar 30 min, or replace |
| Water spraying from fitting | Too much pressure | Check pressure regulator, replace if faulty |
| Timer not triggering | Dead batteries | Replace batteries, use lithium for AL heat/humidity |
| Soggy soil | Too long a run time | Reduce timer duration by 5 min increments |
| Dry soil despite running | Emitter flow too low or clogged | Increase emitter GPH or unclog |
| System runs but plants wilting | Emitters too far from root zone | Move emitter stakes closer to stem base |
| Algae in lines | Lines exposed to sunlight | Cover lines with mulch or use black poly line |
