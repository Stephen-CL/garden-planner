## Caging & Support

- **Tomatoes** — YES, cage required. Use heavy gauge 5–6 ft cages or wooden stakes with twine. Cherokee Purple, Sun Gold, and Big Beef are all indeterminate and will hit the top of standard cages by August. Roma is determinate and a standard cage is fine.
- **Peppers** — NO cage needed. Self-supporting at 2–3 ft. If a branch droops under heavy fruit, tie to a single bamboo stake.
- **Pole beans** — train onto chicken wire arch. No additional support needed.
- **Cucumbers** — train onto chicken wire arch. No additional support needed.
- **Hyacinth bean vine** — train onto chicken wire arch. Very aggressive grower, will self-attach quickly.
- **Okra** — free-standing, no support needed. Grows 5–6 ft tall.

## North Alabama Growing Notes

- **Last frost:** ~March 25 (Madison, AL)
- **First fall frost:** ~November 1–15
- **Summer heat:** Brutal July–August. Choose heat-tolerant varieties. Water at soil level to prevent fungal issues.
- **Humidity:** High — always water at base, never overhead, especially on tomatoes.
- **Soil:** Amend beds each season with 2–3 in of compost. AL clay can be heavy.
- **Nematodes:** Root-knot nematodes are a real problem in AL soil. Nasturtiums and future marigold plantings help suppress them.
- **Common pests to watch:** Tomato hornworms, squash vine borers (Jun–Jul), Japanese beetles, harlequin bugs, aphids.

## Fall Planning Notes

When summer crops wind down in August–September, consider these for fall planting:

- **Garlic** (plant October, harvest June) — great long-term candidate for Bed 4 middle row
- **Cauliflower** — much more reliable in fall than spring in AL. Plant transplants in August.
- **Broccoli** — plant transplants in August, harvest November
- **Kale** — plant August, overwinters well in zone 7b
- **Carrots** — resow in late August for a fall crop, sweetest after first frost
- **Red onions (short-day variety)** — plant sets February–March next season
